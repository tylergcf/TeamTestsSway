What is a Chromebook?

If you're thinking about buying a new computer, you might consider getting a Chromebook instead. A Chromebook is a low-cost alternative to traditional laptops from Google, with several models available for less than $300.

Unlike most computers, Chromebooks do not run Windows. Instead, they use Chrome OS, which is very similar to the Google Chrome web browser. This means you can use a Chromebook to do just about anything you'd normally do on the web: read websites, check your email, watch online videos, and so on.

Is a Chromebook right for you?

For many people, a Chromebook can do just about everything they need for much less than a traditional laptop.

That doesn't mean that a Chromebook is perfect for everyone, though. For example, most Chromebooks don't have very much hard drive space. That's because Google expects you to have consistent Internet access, storing most of your documents, photos, and other files in the cloud.

Another limitation is that Chromebooks use Chrome OS, which means that you can't install traditional Windows software like Microsoft Office or Adobe Photoshop. If you use a lot of third-party software, or like playing the most recent games on your computer, a Chromebook probably won't be able to do everything that you need.

Learning how to use a Chromebook

While a Chromebook isn't all that different from a regular computer, it may take some time to become familiar with the interface. Fortunately, Google has created some excellent how-to guides for new users, and they're really helpful when you're just getting started:

  • Official Chromebook Help Center
  • Chromebook Central User Forms

If you're struggling to work without Windows software like Microsoft Office and Adobe Photoshop, remember that there are free alternatives to these types of software, such as Google Docs , Office Online , and Pixlr which are compatible with Chromebooks. Google provides a nice overview on their Switching to Chromebook page.
